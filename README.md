# IBM HR Analytics – Employee Attrition & Performance

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition using the well-known **IBM HR Analytics dataset**. The primary objective was to uncover the core reasons behind employee turnover (*Attrition*) and identify where the highest risks of workforce instability concentrate within the organization. 

By transforming raw data into an interactive, decision-ready Power BI dashboard, this analysis delivers actionable business recommendations to help HR departments mitigate turnover and improve employee retention.

---

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Methodology
The project follows a comprehensive data analytics workflow:

1. **SQL**: Used for initial data exploration, filtering, and understanding key metrics and distributions.
2. **Power Query**: Handled extensive data cleaning, type casting, transforming data structures, and creating a robust data model.
3. **Power BI**: Built an interactive multi-page dashboard focusing on data visualization, DAX measures, and a dedicated **Key Insights** section for executive summarizing.

---

## 📊 Key Insights & Business Findings

The analysis successfully isolated several major drivers of employee attrition, structured into three critical areas:

### 👤 1. Who is leaving?
* **Age & Marital Status:** Younger and single employees exhibit significantly higher attrition rates, whereas workforce retention steadily increases with age and maturity.
* **Gender:** Men show a slightly higher attrition rate compared to women.
* **Education:** Educational background proved to have little to no measurable impact on employee turnover, suggesting internal organizational factors play a much larger role.

### 🧠 2. Why do they leave?
* **Workplace Environment:** Low job satisfaction and weak professional relationships are primary drivers of departures.
* **The Overtime Trigger:** Frequent overtime is one of the strongest catalysts for attrition, especially when it is combined with low compensation or lack of recognition.
* **Work-Life Balance:** Employees rating their work-life balance poorly are considerably more likely to look for opportunities elsewhere.

### 📍 3. Where risk concentrates
* **Role & Department:** Sales Representatives suffer from the highest attrition rates, while Directors and Managers demonstrate the strongest retention.
* **Development & Travel:** Limited access to training opportunities combined with frequent business travel (*delegations*) heavily correlates with higher turnover rates.

---

## 💡 Executive Recommendations
Based on the dashboard findings, organizations should focus on:
* 🎯 **Targeting Retention:** Developing early-career engagement programs specifically tailored for younger and single staff.
* ⏰ **Overtime & Compensation Audit:** Reviewing workloads, managing burnout, and ensuring fair reward structures for overtime.
* ✈️ **Role Optimization:** Reviewing the sales role structure and optimizing travel loads to reduce employee fatigue.
